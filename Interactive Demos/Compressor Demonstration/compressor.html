This is an interactive demonstration of a compressor. Load a sound file and then vary the Threshold and Ratio controls to get a feel for how they work. The effect can be particularly pronounced on drum loops.

(particularly, try setting the Threshold low and the Ratio high, and see what it does to the sound)

Compressors work by reducing the amplitude of a signal when it is above a set Threshold. Above that threshold, the signal will be attenuated by a specific Ratio. In this case our Ratio can be set from 1:1 (no attenuation) to 20:1 (strong attenuation).

Because they attenuate the signal, it's common for compressors to also include a Make-up Gain control, to compensate for the overall reduction in amplitude. Try imposing heavy compression on the signal, then raise the overall gain with the Make-up Gain slider.
